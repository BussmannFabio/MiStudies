SMALL TALK

Do you have problems when you meet people for the first time? Is it difficult to think of what to talk about?

Don't worry. You don't need to talk about yourself; ask the right questions and you can make the other person talk.

→ This article is for: people who find it difficult to start or keep conversations, especially with strangers.

4. Do you play any sports? Which ones? What was the last song you listened to?

When more relaxed, ask about relationships and home life:

Where did you grow up? Are you married? Do you have any children?
5. Do you live near here?

You can ask about work and school anytime:

6. What do you do? or Where do you study?

⚠️ Be careful – sometimes people don't want to talk about work at a party!

🚫 Topics to AVOID:
Money: 7. How much do you earn? or 8. How much rent do you pay? — people usually think talking about money is rude.
Politics & Religion — you don't want to start an argument!
Age — never guess anyone's age!

📐 Rules Summary 1. With be: the verb comes before the subject.
2. With other verbs: an auxiliary verb (do/does/did/can) goes before the subject.
3. With do/did: the main verb stays in the base form.
Does she live here? ✅   Does she lives here? ❌
💬 Tip: be like / How + be
When asking for a description or opinion, use:

be like → What was the film like? — It was all right.
How + be → How was your vacation? — Fantastic!
b Answer the grammar reflection questions
  • In questions with the verb be, which word comes first: be or the subject? → be comes first
  • In questions with other main verbs, what kind of word goes before the subject? → An auxiliary verb (do, does, did, can…)
